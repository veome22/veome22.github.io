Research

I am interested in using rapid population synthesis to probe the origins and distributions of Double Compact Objects (DCOs) in the universe.

Rapid Population Synthesis

We can use semi-analytic stellar evolution models to generate DCO populations with various physical assumptions.

Supernova Kicks

When stars end their evolution in a supernova, they receive a recoil 'kick'. The direction and magnitude of the kick can determine if the newly formed compact object stays in a binary system to eventually merge with its companion. Additionally, the kick imparts an angular momentum to the binary, and can lead to spin precession. The distribution of these supernova kicks is crucial to characterizing the population of DCOs.

Gravitational Wave Modeling Systematics

The waveform models used for parameter estimation of DCO mergers have finite precision and accuracy. With upcoming graviational wave detectors, a lack of accurate waveform models can lead to significant biases in recovered parameters.
